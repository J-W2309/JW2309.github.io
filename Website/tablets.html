Tablets


Tablets have become quite popular in recent times due to their flexability, they are a combination of your Smart Phone and a Laptop. To put it simply it is like having a larger mobile phone. they use the same operating systems and are usually manufactured by the same companies creating phones. Due to this a lot of the security and advice to keeping secure is similar to smart phones.


Phyiscal Security Risks


Physical

Physcially tablets are larger than your mobile phone but still one of the smaller smart device's you may have in your home. the same rules apply, keep an eye on your device dont misplace it and keep it safe. like many smart devices tablets can be a target for theft.



Technical Security Risks


Passwords

One of the first things you will do when settings up a Tablet is choosing your Password/Pin. Most newer Tablets now use finger prints or facial recognition for this instead which is much more secure however it is still important to make sure that whatever Password or Pin you choose is secure as it is a solid second line of defense against anyone trying to access your device.

Updates

Keeping your Tablet updated is important to keep your device secure, Updates will include the latest security and fixes for not only your device but your apps. Making sure you are up to date is one of the most important steps to keeping your smart device safe.

Wi-Fi

When it comes to Smart Devices Wi-Fi is one of the most important aspects. it is also one that needs to have a close eye kept on.

Apps

It goes without saying that you should be careful what Apps you are downloading onto your Mobile Device. The majority of Apps are most likely safe and the App stores do monitor what is on there available however due to the sheer volume of apps its not always possible to notice malicious apps on first sight. Whenever downloading an App on your Tablet make sure that... One: it is a trusted manufacturer and within the App store. Two: That it isnt a decoy, some apps may take the apperance of another to trick the user into downloading. make sure you double check the Apps you are downloading to avoid this.

Hacking

We are all aware of Hackers and Tablets are a major target. Due to their high value and the amount of information they store. There are a lot of different attacks hackers may try to pull. too many to outline here but most advice boils down to the simple stuff. Dont click and links that look suspicious, be careful who you give your information to, dont share any log in details etc. This guide will not go over every attack that could occour however there are many external tests that have been conducted and published on these devices. If you would like to read more visit the "published tests" section below.

Browsers

Browsers can vary depending on the device you use, however most are available to download on any. Some people prefer to use alternative Browsers that they are comfortable with. this is fine as long as you trust them and they are reputable. The main risk with Browsers is the information they store. Browsers themselves similar to the device will store a lot of your personal information for later use. as an example Google Chrome may store your passwords within the browser for ease of use. This is a very nifty feature however not always safe. The general rule of thumb is to be careful what you give your browser permission to see or use as well as pick a browser that is well known or that you know is secure.
